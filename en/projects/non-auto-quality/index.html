Risk · Jan 2021—Apr 2023

Non-auto Portfolio Quality

Linking prevention, portfolio signals and claims learning into one risk-management loop.

Context

Personal non-auto insurance spans products and channels with different risk signatures. Fragmented controls make it difficult to see whether risk is shifting or whether an intervention is working.

Challenge

The program had to connect controls before underwriting, monitoring during the policy period and evidence after claims—without treating any single rule or model as the full answer.

My Role

I coordinated the quality and risk-control program, including budget planning, framework design and cross-functional delivery.

Approach

We organized signals by decision point, identified the action each signal should trigger, and created a feedback path from claims findings into future underwriting controls.

Execution

The work combined targeted real-time controls, portfolio monitoring, claims collaboration on suspicious segments and regular review of intervention effectiveness.

Outcomes

Contemporaneous project materials recorded approximately RMB 9 million of program investment and about RMB 30 million in loss reduction for 2022. These figures are presented only under the confirmed public wording approved before launch; detailed assumptions and internal calculations remain withheld.

Lessons

Risk control is strongest as a learning system. Every rule needs an owner, a measurable intended effect, and a route for outcome evidence to refine the next decision.
