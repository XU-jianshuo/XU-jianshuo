Non-auto Cost Management

A consistent language for assessing cost before a decision, monitoring it in operation and learning afterward.

Context

Different products and channels often describe cost from different perspectives. Without consistent definitions, teams can make locally reasonable decisions that do not add up at portfolio level.

Challenge

The task was to create a framework useful for both technical assessment and operating action, while respecting the timing differences between written business, earned exposure and emerging claims.

My Role

I designed and advanced the non-auto cost measurement framework, established assessment principles and connected them to business monitoring and review.

Approach

The model separates decision-time estimates from observed development, specifies comparable views for quotation, policy and overall performance, and assigns each measure to a management question.

Execution

Pre-assessment supported proposed business decisions; in-process monitoring surfaced emerging divergence; post-review compared assumptions, actions and outcomes to improve subsequent decisions.

Outcomes

The framework established a shared cost-management chain across actuarial and operating teams. The public account deliberately omits internal targets, thresholds and the previously undefined percentage metric.

Lessons

Measurement frameworks gain trust when definitions, timing and limitations are visible. Review should explain not only what moved, but which assumption or action needs to change next.
